# Managed Services Ticket Categorization Guidelines

**Categorize as "Managed Services" if a ticket involves any of the following:**

- **User or Client Onboarding and Offboarding:**  
  - Creating, configuring, or removing accounts, permissions, and devices for employees or client users.  
  - Examples: new employee onboarding, terminating user access, preparing accounts and permissions for a new client environment.

- **Email Issues and Support:**  
  - Troubleshooting problems with email services, delivery, mailbox configuration, or account access.  
  - Examples: users unable to send or receive email, mailbox syncing issues, shared mailbox access problems, or Outlook configuration issues.

- **Password Resets:**  
  - Requests to reset, unlock, or change passwords when a user cannot access their account.  
  - Examples: locked accounts, expired passwords, forgotten credentials, or authentication problems.

- **Spam Filtering and Email Security:**  
  - Requests related to spam management, blocked senders, or filtering configuration.  
  - Examples: legitimate emails being marked as spam, adjusting filtering rules, or reviewing quarantine messages.

- **Network Outages or Connectivity Problems:**  
  - Issues involving loss of internet access, LAN connectivity failures, or disruptions affecting multiple systems or users.  
  - Examples: office network outages, Wi-Fi failures, VPN connectivity problems, or site-wide connectivity disruptions.

- **Software Installation or Updates:**  
  - Installing approved applications, updating existing software, or applying patches to supported systems.  
  - Examples: installing business applications, updating productivity software, or deploying security updates.

- **New Device Requests (Existing Managed Environment):**  
  - Requests for new hardware or device setup within an existing managed environment.  
  - Examples: requesting a replacement laptop, adding a workstation for an existing employee, or preparing a device already owned by the client.  
  - Do **not** include requests that represent new hardware purchases through sales.

- **Hardware Failure or Malfunction:**  
  - Issues where existing equipment is failing, malfunctioning, or requires repair or replacement.  
  - Examples: failing hard drives, broken monitors, malfunctioning peripherals, or systems that no longer power on.

- **Backup Failures or Backup Management Issues:**  
  - Problems involving backup systems, failed backup jobs, restore requests, or backup monitoring alerts.  
  - Examples: backup jobs failing overnight, restore requests for lost files, or backup agents reporting errors.

- **Server or Workstation Maintenance:**  
  - Routine maintenance tasks performed on managed infrastructure or endpoints.  
  - Examples: patching servers, performing system updates, scheduled maintenance tasks, or workstation health checks.

---

**Managed Services is NOT:**

- Sales-related hardware or service procurement requests for devices the client does not yet own  
- New infrastructure design or project-based implementations  
- Security incident investigations or breach response  
- Non-technical account or billing inquiries  
- Large-scale project work that involves deploying entirely new environments or services

---

**Examples:**

- “Please onboard a new employee starting Monday.”  
- “I can’t log into my email; it keeps rejecting my password.”  
- “Our office internet is down.”  
- “Can you install Zoom on my workstation?”  
- “Our backups failed last night.”  
- “My laptop’s hard drive appears to be failing.”  
- “Please prepare a workstation for an existing employee.”

---

**Note:**  
Categorize as **Managed Services** when the request involves routine IT support, maintenance, troubleshooting, or operational tasks within an existing managed environment. These tickets typically relate to user access, system functionality, device health, software management, or ongoing infrastructure support rather than sales, project work, or new infrastructure creation.
