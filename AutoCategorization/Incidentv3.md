# Incident Ticket Categorization Guidelines

**Categorize as "Incident" if a ticket involves any of the following:**

- **General Errors and Failures:**  
  - Any issue where something is not working as expected, is producing an error, or has stopped functioning.  
  - Examples: error messages, failed processes, unexpected behavior, or inability to complete a task.

- **Application Errors and Crashes:**  
  - Problems involving software that is crashing, freezing, not opening, or displaying errors.  
  - Examples: line-of-business applications failing to launch, programs closing unexpectedly, or applications becoming unresponsive.

- **Hardware Failures:**  
  - Issues where a physical device is malfunctioning, damaged, or no longer operating correctly.  
  - Examples: computers not powering on, failing hard drives, broken laptops, or docking stations not working.

- **Peripheral Failures:**  
  - Problems affecting connected devices or accessories that are not functioning properly.  
  - Examples: printers not printing, monitors not displaying, keyboards or mice failing, webcams not working.

- **Performance Issues:**  
  - Situations where a device or system is functioning poorly or slower than expected.  
  - Examples: computer slowness, delayed response times, freezing, or severe lag affecting work.

- **Email and Communication Issues:**  
  - Problems with sending, receiving, or accessing email and related communication tools.  
  - Examples: inability to send or receive email, mailbox access failures, or email client errors.

- **Internet and Connectivity Issues:**  
  - Problems involving loss of internet access, unstable connections, or inability to reach required resources.  
  - Examples: internet down, network interruptions, Wi-Fi failures, or inability to connect to company systems.

- **Security-Related Issues:**  
  - Incidents involving suspicious, potentially malicious, or unsafe activity that may require investigation.  
  - Examples: suspicious emails, phishing messages, unusual login behavior, or possible unauthorized access.

- **Authentication and Access Failures:**  
  - Situations where a user should already have access, but access is failing because something is not working correctly.  
  - Examples: password not working, login failures, account lockouts, or access suddenly being denied.

**An Incident is NOT:**

- Requests for new software, hardware, or services  
- User onboarding, account creation, or planned setup work  
- Access requests for systems the user does not already have  
- Recommendations, advisory requests, or general how-to questions  
- Routine changes, installations, or upgrades where nothing is broken  

**Examples:**

- “Password not working”  
- “Computer is slow”  
- “Line of business application is crashing or not opening”  
- “Can’t send/receive email”  
- “Internet down”  
- “Suspicious email”  

**Note:**  
Categorize as **Incident** when the user is reporting that something is broken, failing, unavailable, degraded, or behaving unexpectedly. The defining trait is that a system, device, application, account, or service is not working as intended and requires troubleshooting or restoration.
